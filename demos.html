Demos

Explore practical demonstrations of Space Station OS in action. These demos showcase various features and capabilities in simulated environments.

Demo 1 - Nauka Incident

This demo recreates the ISS Nauka incident for fault analysis. Follow these steps:

  1. Run the following commands in separate terminals after sourcing your ROS 2 environment:
    • ros2 run space_station_gnc demo1a_nauka_incident_estimate - Terminal 1
    • ros2 launch space_station_gnc launch_gnc.py - Terminal 2
    • ros2 run rviz2 rviz2 - Terminal 3 (Launches RVIZ)
  2. Observe the simulation and verify the response process.

Prospective Demos

Demo P1 - Dynamic Thermal Management

Scenario: During an unexpected solar flare, the space station experiences an increase in heat load on specific modules, threatening to overheat sensitive equipment. The thermal management system must dynamically adjust coolant flow and redistribute heat to maintain optimal operating temperatures.

User Story: As a systems engineer, I want the digital twin to simulate and monitor the thermal behavior of the space station in real time, so that I can dynamically adjust the thermal management systems to handle unexpected events such as solar flares or equipment overheating, ensuring the safety and functionality of onboard systems.

Acceptance Criteria:

  • Real-time thermal distribution maps are generated.
  • Automated or manual control adjustments can be simulated and executed.
  • Alerts are provided for anomalies, such as extreme temperature variations.
  • Post-event analysis tools evaluate the response's effectiveness.

Requirements:

  • Inputs: Solar flare intensity, thermal sensor data, heat exchanger performance, station orientation.
  • Outputs: Temperature distribution maps, coolant system adjustments, alerts for critical zones.
  • Simulation Items: Thermal models of station modules, heat transfer dynamics, coolant system behavior under varying loads.
  • Potential Subsystems: Thermal, electrical (power loads affecting heat generation), and structural (thermal expansion).

Demo P2 - Collision Avoidance and Debris Tracking

Scenario: A piece of orbital debris is detected on a potential collision course with the space station. The system must simulate the impact trajectory, predict collision likelihood, and generate an evasive maneuver plan to avoid the debris.

User Story: As a mission operator, I want the digital twin to predict and simulate potential collisions with orbital debris, so that I can plan and execute evasive maneuvers effectively, minimizing risk to the station.

Acceptance Criteria:

  • Integration of real-time orbital debris data.
  • Prediction of collision probability with confidence intervals.
  • Maneuver plans are simulated for resource impact (fuel, time).
  • Reports are generated post-maneuver to evaluate risk reduction.

Requirements:

  • Inputs: Orbital debris trajectory data, station position and velocity, maneuver capabilities, fuel reserves.
  • Outputs: Collision risk assessment, maneuver trajectory, fuel consumption estimates.
  • Simulation Items: Orbital mechanics models, debris tracking algorithms, station propulsion dynamics.
  • Potential Subsystems: Guidance, navigation, and control (GNC); propulsion; thermal (maneuver heat loads); and communication (maneuver updates to ground).

Demo P3 - Life Support System Anomalies

Scenario: A sudden failure in the CO₂ scrubbing unit increases carbon dioxide levels in the crew's living quarters. The system must simulate the propagation of CO₂ levels and recommend corrective actions to stabilize the environment.

User Story: As an onboard astronaut, I want the digital twin to simulate the life support system’s behavior under anomalous conditions, such as oxygen generator failure or CO₂ scrubber malfunction, so that I can ensure the station remains habitable while troubleshooting or performing repairs.

Acceptance Criteria:

  • Simulations of critical component failures in life support systems.
  • Real-time monitoring of environmental parameters (e.g., oxygen, CO₂ levels).
  • Decision support for manual interventions or automated adjustments.
  • Alerts for thresholds that jeopardize crew safety.

Requirements:

  • Inputs: CO₂ concentration sensors, airflow data, life support component status.
  • Outputs: Real-time CO₂ concentration maps, recommended corrective actions (e.g., manual adjustments, system reconfigurations).
  • Simulation Items: Life support system models, airflow distribution, crew metabolic rates.
  • Potential Subsystems: Environmental control and life support (ECLSS), electrical, thermal (airflow impact on heat distribution).

Demo P4 - Fuel Consumption Optimization

Scenario: The station requires an emergency repositioning to avoid a collision with an unexpected object. The system must determine the optimal maneuver that minimizes fuel consumption while ensuring safety.

User Story: As a flight director, I want the digital twin to model fuel consumption for station-keeping and unexpected repositioning, so that I can optimize fuel usage and extend operational lifespan.

Acceptance Criteria:

  • Accurate fuel consumption predictions for planned and emergency maneuvers.
  • Evaluation of alternative strategies for minimal fuel use.
  • Visualizations of fuel reserves and usage impact on station operations.
  • Alerts when fuel reserves approach critical levels.

Requirements:

  • Inputs: Station position and velocity, maneuver requirements, propulsion system status, fuel reserves.
  • Outputs: Optimal maneuver trajectory, fuel consumption estimates, impact on future station-keeping.
  • Simulation Items: Propulsion system dynamics, fuel usage models, orbital mechanics.
  • Potential Subsystems: GNC, propulsion, thermal (maneuver-induced heat loads), and structural (stress from maneuvers).

Demo P5 - Simulated Emergency Evacuation

Scenario: A fire breaks out in one of the station modules, triggering alarms and requiring crew evacuation to designated safe zones. The system must simulate escape routes and recommend the safest and fastest evacuation plan.

User Story: As a safety officer, I want the digital twin to simulate emergency evacuation scenarios, such as fires or depressurization, so that I can ensure crew safety through optimized escape routes and evacuation procedures.

Acceptance Criteria:

  • Real-time simulations of various emergency scenarios.
  • Visualization of optimal evacuation routes under dynamic conditions.
  • Evaluation of crew safety during simulated emergencies.
  • Feedback reports on procedural efficiency and bottlenecks.

Requirements:

  • Inputs: Fire location, crew locations, station layout, hatch status.
  • Outputs: Recommended evacuation routes, time estimates, potential bottlenecks.
  • Simulation Items: Fire propagation models, station layout and accessibility, crew movement dynamics.
  • Potential Subsystems: Structural, environmental (smoke and fire), communication (crew coordination), and life support.

Demo P6 - Structural Integrity and Vibration Monitoring

Scenario: A micro-meteoroid strikes the station, causing localized stress and vibrations. The system must analyze the impact's effects on structural integrity and recommend repair or reinforcement actions.

User Story: As a structural engineer, I want the digital twin to model and monitor the station’s structural integrity under abnormal stresses, such as micro-meteoroid impacts or excessive vibration, so that I can evaluate and mitigate risks to the station’s long-term safety.

Acceptance Criteria:

  • Real-time structural stress and vibration analysis.
  • Simulation of stress responses under various impact scenarios.
  • Recommendations for structural reinforcements or repairs.
  • Alerts for critical thresholds that risk structural failure.

Requirements:

  • Inputs: Impact data (location, velocity, mass), vibration sensors, structural material properties.
  • Outputs: Stress distribution maps, vibration decay analysis, repair recommendations.
  • Simulation Items: Structural models, impact dynamics, vibration propagation.
  • Potential Subsystems: Structural, thermal (localized heating from impact), and communication (alerts to ground and crew).

Demo P7 - Predictive Maintenance for Critical Systems

Scenario: The station’s power distribution system shows signs of degradation, with intermittent faults in one of the primary circuits. The system must predict when the fault will likely cause a failure and recommend preemptive maintenance.

User Story: As a maintenance planner, I want the digital twin to predict failures in critical systems, such as power and communication subsystems, so that I can plan preemptive repairs and avoid operational disruptions.

Acceptance Criteria:

  • Predictive models for system degradation and failure probabilities.
  • Simulations of repair or replacement impacts on operations.
  • Real-time alerts for impending failures.
  • Maintenance schedules optimized for resource and time efficiency.

Requirements:

  • Inputs: Power usage logs, fault occurrence data, component wear and tear models.
  • Outputs: Failure probability forecasts, maintenance schedules, spare part requirements.
  • Simulation Items: Power system degradation models, fault diagnostics, repair simulation.
  • Potential Subsystems: Electrical, communication (diagnostic data relay), and structural (maintenance access points).
